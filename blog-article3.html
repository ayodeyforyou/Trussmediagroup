WHY GEN Z IS RESHAPING AFRICAN CONTENT CONSUMPTION

May 2025 • by Adewale S. Adekunle

The way African content is consumed has changed dramatically and Gen Zs, individuals born between 1997 and 2010, are at the center of that transformation. Across Nigeria and the continent, this generation is not just consuming content they are actively shaping what is made, how it is made, how it is distributed and what stories gain attention. They are driving a bold new era where storytelling is mobile, authentic, and deeply interactive.

Mobile first, Always

For Gen Z, the phone isn’t just a device, it is their primary screen and source of entertainment. Traditional TV is no longer the heartbeat of entertainment. This generation spends most of their time scrolling, streaming and sharing content on the go. They are not waiting for prime-time, they create their own prime-time moments. This shift is pushing content creators and production companies to design stories that are quick, platform-fluid and visually engaging right from the first few seconds and this is what we can craft at Truss Media Group because in the fast-moving world of short-form videos and social storytelling, capturing and holding Gen Z’s attention is both a challenge and an opportunity.

Authenticity Over Perfection

More than any generation before them, Gen Z craves authenticity. They can spot a forced, staged, inauthentic and over-produced story from a mile away. The are not just your regular consumer as they don’t just want to watch, they also want to feel connected. Stories that highlight real people, real struggles, real contemporary issues and honest cultural experiences win their loyalty. For storytellers, this means leaning into the raw, the local, the human. Gen Z doesn’t need perfection, they want something that feels real, even if it’s messy. Brands and creators who embrace this will find a powerful, engaged audience. For Truss Media Group, this is not a challenge, it is our sweet spot. We specialize in crafting bold, authentic stories that resonate across Nigeria and the African diaspora. Through our screenwriting services, we develop narratives that speak directly to the heart of Gen Z’s experiences which includes social justice, identity, love, mental health, and more.

Representation Matters and it matters Deeply

Diversity is non-negotiable for Gen Z. They want to see stories that reflect their realities, their languages, their communities, their style, their slangs, their subcultures. African audiences, in particular, are demanding content that shows Africa in its full complex beauty not just the simplified versions they have often been served. Creators who tell unapologetically African stories are finally finding their voices heard not just on the continent but on global stages. Stories rooted in local culture when told well have universal appeal. They travel, they resonate and they inspire.

It Is About Community, Not Just Content

For Gen Z, content doesn’t exist in isolation, it lives within communities. They build conversations around the films, series, and moments that matter to them. They share, remix, debate and celebrate their favorite stories together. This is why storytelling today must spark participation. Whether it’s through social media, fan-driven content, or interactive campaigns, the goal is to build a community not just an audience. The most successful content today creates spaces for Gen Z to engage, not just consume.

Why It Matters for Brands and Creators

The content economy is moving to the rhythm of Gen Z. Understanding their preferences, their platforms and their values is essential for anyone who wants to make an impact in today’s African media landscape. This generation is steering the future of storytelling Now and Beyond. They are shaping what is popular, what gets funded and what gets shared. The brands, creators and production teams who listen and evolve with them will find themselves not just relevant but leading.

Final Thoughts

Gen Z isn’t waiting for permission to shape the future of African content which they are already doing. They want stories that reflect their world. They aren’t here to watch from the sidelines. They are actively creating, curating and reshaping what content means on the continent.
